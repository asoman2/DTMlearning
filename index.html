Historical Data Intelligence & Critical Assessment
Nine years of QBDT data contains an extraordinary amount of predictive signal that Meridian has never seen in aggregate — because it has always been siloed across 10 disconnected files. This document surfaces what that data almost certainly shows (Pareto job profitability, structural cash compression, retainage collection cycles, labour cost by state) and delivers an honest critical opinion about the business: the Controller is a single point of failure, LCPtracker must start Month 2, and the three Month-1 decisions that determine whether this migration succeeds or fails.

Progress Tracking & Acceptability Strategy
Progress is not completion. Acceptability is progress. This document defines how both axes are tracked simultaneously — technical completion (has the work been done?) and participant acceptability (do the people believe it is working for them?) — and why divergence between the two is the real risk metric. Covers the 4-state divergence matrix, per-persona acceptability definitions pre-agreed in Month 1, 3 signal types (Behavioural 50% / Data comparison 35% / Voice of participant 15%), acceptability gates per wave, the 4-threshold divergence escalation model, and 8 Voice of Participant moments.

Education 3 phases · 9 delivery formats · Champion-led
Self-Serve User Education Strategy
The governing education strategy across three phases — pre-migration (curiosity before anxiety), during migration (just-in-time not just-in-case), and post go-live (capability build, not catch-up). Establishes the benefit-first principle that shapes all education: never ask someone to learn something before they understand why their life will be better because of it. Covers cognitive load budgets per persona, the Champion network as the highest-leverage investment, 9 delivery formats matched to context and device, 8 Voice of Participant moments, and 6 design principles for the education system.

Part 3
Education Interactive · Role-adaptive · AI-done-for-you
Concept Translation Education Layer
The interactive education experience for the four core QBDT → IES concept translations: Customer:Jobs → Projects, Classes & Locations → Dimensions, and Price Levels → Price Rules. Built on the Discover → Understand in context → Confirm sequence — the cognitive model is inverted from traditional training: AI has already mapped all 187 Meridian jobs, 9 segment values, and 42 client pricing assignments. Users confirm what AI has done, not configure from scratch. Role selector (CFO / Controller / PM / Estimator / Payroll Manager) adapts every aha moment and construction use case to the user's specific daily work. All examples use real Meridian project data: Copper Ridge, CalTrans I-15, Midtown Office Fit-out, NV DOT US-95.

Part 3
Education 5 tiers · 210 employees · Full workforce
IES Education Rollout — Full Workforce Strategy
Extends the DSU model beyond the migration team to all 210 Meridian employees — including those who never log into IES. Defines 5 workforce tiers by IES relationship (not org chart position): Power users (35), Regular users (60), Light users (15), Self-service users (115), and IES-ambient users (40+) who are affected by IES but never touch it. For each tier: a calibrated DSU sequence, the right channel and format, wave-aligned rollout timing, language and literacy adaptations, and a behavioural success metric. Includes the IES-ambient education strategy — oral delivery, foreman script, bilingual printed card, union steward co-delivery for Wave 3.
