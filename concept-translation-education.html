UX strategy note
Why "done for you" changes how concept translation works
▼
The cognitive sequence is inverted. Traditional migration education: learn the concept → understand the mapping → configure from scratch. Done-for-you: discover what AI has already mapped in your file → understand the mapping in the context of your own data → confirm or adjust. The user never configures from scratch. They recognise their own projects, classes, and clients in the new structure and confirm. Education happens through recognition, not recall.

Why role-specific framing matters here specifically. "Customer:Jobs map to Projects" means entirely different things to a Project Manager (who cares about job cost reporting), a Controller (who cares about WIP accounting), and an Estimator (who cares about bid-to-budget workflow). The concept is the same. The consequence for daily work is completely different. One explanation cannot serve all three — so the AI generates role-specific "what changes for you" framings from the same underlying mapping data.

Construction industry conceptual layer. Every example uses real Meridian project names, real client relationships, and real construction workflows (AIA billing, certified payroll, WIP schedules, prevailing wage). Abstract concepts like "Dimensions" become concrete when the example is "Division: Government → all your CalTrans and municipal contracts" rather than "Dimensions allow multi-segment reporting."
